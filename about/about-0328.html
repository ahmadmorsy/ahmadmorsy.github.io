Short Version

Born in the United States, but grown in Egypt, I developed a special taste for mixed art and design. My bachelor in engineering influenced the way I look at shapes, the techniques I follow and the logic behind my design choices. My passion for patterns and geometry took me on a journey of self-discovery. I found in me, an engineer, an artist, a designer and they blended gracefully with my recent interest in web development and programming. You can find more details about my journey in the timeline below, but if you feel this version wasn't short enough, drop me a message and let's get creative.

Timeline

  • 2008

    • AIN SHAMS UNIVERSITY - Egypt

      Faculty of Engineering

      Degree: Bachelor of Electrical Power Engineering

      Experience: Beside the great knowledge I've acquired about electrical systems, and how such systems connect everything our technology is based on today, I also gained the great skill of problem solving. Engineers are taught, programmed to be accurate, to solve whatever problems they face. Some people would call me stubborn, because whatever situation I find myself in, I keep applying the trial-and-error technique till I find a solution.

    • MET - Egypt

      Technical Engineer

      Responsibilities: I was responible for designing UI/UX for touch screens and SCADA systems and working with engineers from the control department to successfully connect such systems to their programmable logic controller (PLC) units. MET is the TOSHIBA system integrator in Egypt that implements control systems in big factories and power plants such as Nestlé.

      Experience: Beside the knowledge I've gained from being out in the actual world and dealing with existing problems beyond the folds of the books we studied in school, I've also developed a good bunch of social skills as part of the job required meeting managers and maintenance engineers to discuss how my company can help them grow their business and enhance the quality of their production.

  • 2012

    • FREELANCE

      Graphic Designer

      The environment that my dad grew up in, and the traditions he was raised upon, made him the strong person he is today, but formed a barrier between him and each one of us. I vividly remember the tense conversation, developing into a big argument, between me and my dad when I expressed my strong passion for art and my wish to study art and design once I'm done with high school. My dad's dream of my becoming an engineer were very evident during my years of study but that day they were strongly expressed and they developed into a final decision. As mature as I am today, I understand why he had such dreams and why he was very strict about them, but I also wish he knew that our times, generations and circumstances were totally different. The closed-minded beliefs that everyone should either be an engineer or has a medical profession wasn't there anymore.

      Following the traditions, and obeying what my dad decided regarding my future, I joined the faculty of engineering. As the years went by, I had no other choice but being a good student and working hard for good grades. Soon, what was once forced upon me, became a part of who I am. My years of study, along with the working years in the engineering field developed my way of thinking.

    • Company

      Job Title

      I’ve always been an observant to art and design. The geometric shapes and oriental patterns I was surrounded with, due to my background, developed my interest in art and my passion for all its forms. In 2012, I quit my engineering job, and dropped everything else to focus entirely on graphic design. I started reading and watching tutorials to learn as much as I could. Ever since then, I’ve experimented with everything visually creative, from drawing and painting to sculpture and collage-making. I became a better designer and built a relatively good base through working in few design firms and art studios. I was happy that I'm on the path that I've always wanted for myself, but I felt that I'm standing still. A change, rather a deviation, was highly needed.

    • Company

      Job Title

      In 2015, I took a decision to go back to my birthplace, Philadelphia. I joined school to have an official degree in graphic design to add to my resume. During my study, I worked in a custom framing shop where I learned about the different framing and mounting techniques. I re-designed their logo, and created different branding and marketing materials to add a professional image that wasn't associated with this kind of business before. I became a part-time designer at the framing store, and gained few good customers who had opportunities for more freelance work. It was easier for me to focus on school due to the flexibility of my work hours.
