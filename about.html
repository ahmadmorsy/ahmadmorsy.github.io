A B O U T

Early years

Before July 2015, I didn't remember much about the city I was born in. I knew that it's called Philadelphia, that it's one of the popular and big cities in the united states, and that my dad was a good chef at a greek restaurant in the heart of the city. I remember my brother, nine years older, was very popular among his friends, which I never doubted. He was and still the funny, social and smart one among the three of us. I remember my sister, two years older, with her beautiful smile, when she's not crying, and how close we are. We always had our differences in the way of thinking and how we perceived the world around us, yet we were always connected as if we were one. Parallel to this, there was the main event, the person who tailored and paved the way for us to become what and who we are today,.. mom. Being a very hard working student, a smart wife and a practical mother, she managed to create the perfect balance between her role as a graduate student with endless hours of research and writing to finish her masters degree, and her role as a mother and a wife.

First Transition

I don't remember much about Philadelphia. My memory is very fuzzy when it comes to the first four years. However, I have crystal clear memories of my childhood, and different stages of my life that were formed and crafted beyond the seas. It was time for my parents, to go back to their families, and beloved ones, and to continue what they started. It was time for my mom to pass along the great knowledge she gained while being away for ten years and for my dad to take a break after years of hard work and to observe, set rules, force boundaries, and build fences around us that, later in life, I learned they were needed back then, but definitely, in much smaller doses.

Egypt

I didn't take me long to grasp the Arabic language, and I didn't forget my first spoken language which is English. The life style and the culture that my brother, sister and I adopted have always been a mixture or a blend of two cultures.

Raised in Egypt, I learned how and when to be friendly and social and the situations where a strict attitude and serious face are required. I developed the skill of filtering every input to my senses in a way that serves me the best. I've always analyzed what I read or heard, learning what adds to my knowledge and filtering out the extra non-relevant information. I learned when to strictly follow rules and when I need to find my way around things and to not be very literal about what I hear. I grew up as a sensitive person, who listens to others, empathizes when it's needed and who can be supportive during tough times. I gained few qualities that i'm proud of, such as honesty, being a good friend, loyalty and hospitality. However, being exposed to and interested in western culture, added to and nourished these qualities. Having English as my second language in Egypt (though technically, it is my first language) provided me with confidence and helped me stand out from others in school, at college and even later on in job interviews. Being born in the states and connected to its culture and history developed very useful skills but my personality was primarily shaped by the years I spent in Egypt, at least then.

Engineering and Traditions

The environment that my dad grew up in, and the traditions he was raised upon, made him the strong person he is today, but formed a barrier between him and each one of us. I vividly remember the tense conversation, developing into a big argument, between me and my dad when I expressed my strong passion for art and my wish to study art and design once I'm done with high school. My dad's dream of my becoming an engineer were very evident during my years of study but that day they were strongly expressed and they developed into a final decision. As mature as I am today, I understand why he had such dreams and why he was very strict about them, but I also wish he knew that our times, generations and circumstances were totally different. The closed-minded beliefs that everyone should either be an engineer or has a medical profession wasn't there anymore.

Following the traditions, and obeying what my dad decided regarding my future, I joined the faculty of engineering. As the years went by, I had no other choice but being a good student and working hard for good grades. Soon, what was once forced upon me, became a part of who I am. My years of study, along with the working years in the engineering field developed my way of thinking. The great skill I gained from that experience was problem solving. Engineers are taught, programmed to be accurate, to solve whatever problems they face. Some people call me stubborn, because whatever situation I find myself in, I keep applying the trial-and-error technique till I find a solution.

Art

I’ve always been an observant to art and design. The geometric shapes and oriental patterns I was surrounded with, due to my background, developed my interest in art and my passion for all its forms. In 2012, I quit my engineering job, and dropped everything else to focus entirely on graphic design. I started reading and watching tutorials to learn as much as I could. Ever since then, I’ve experimented with everything visually creative, from drawing and painting to sculpture and collage-making. I became a better designer and built a relatively good base through working in few design firms and art studios. I was happy that I'm on the path that I've always wanted for myself, but I felt that I'm standing still. A change, rather a deviation, was highly needed.

Another Transition

In 2015, I took a decision to go back to my birthplace, Philadelphia. I joined school to have an official degree in graphic design to add to my resume. During my study, I worked in a custom framing shop where I learned about the different framing and mounting techniques. I re-designed their logo, and created different branding and marketing materials to add a professional image that wasn't associated with this kind of business before. I became a part-time designer at the framing store, and gained few good customers who had opportunities for more freelance work. It was easier for me to focus on school due to the flexibility of my work hours.

Today

At the time of writing this, I am taking a break from school to take care of my portfolio. An interest in development and programming appeared out of nowhere, maybe it was buried somewhere there. I started reading and learning HTML, CSS and JS while applying what I learned to my website. I'm almost done with my website, first website that I actually build from scratch, so I'm really excited about how it will turn. I may have added too much effects, transitions, animations and DEFINITELY text but try to overlook them (Hey! it's my first website). As final words, let me add some cliche paragraph that I see a lot in design portfolios, just in this exact spot:

When I’m not designing, I’m probably holding a piece of paper and folding it in different ways that would inspire me (but I honestly, do that.. I really love paper.). If you’re interested in working together or just want to say hi, feel free to reach out. Maybe we can fold paper together.
